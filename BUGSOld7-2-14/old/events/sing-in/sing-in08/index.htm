BUGS will be holding its third annual Sing-In on April 26th in the Steinert Choral Room on the corner of Hope and Power street.  On the menu this year: The Sorcerer, Patience, Iolanthe, The Mikado, the Act I Finale of Yeomen and the close of Act II of Pirates.  Registration is $10 ($5 for students!) and includes coffee, bagels, and pizza.  Interested parties should fill out the registration form, indicating any roles they wish to sing.  For a list of available roles, click here.

If you would be interested in playing piano for one of the shows, not only will we waive your registration fee, you'll earn our undying adulation.

What exactly is a sing-in?
A long-standing G&S tradition, sing-ins are community gatherings where Savoyards sing through works from the canon with piano accompaniment.  There is no dialog and little or no movement (although people are certainly welcome to act if they wish).  Everyone sings the choral parts, often without regard to gender, while leads are cast in advance.  One of the chief goals of the sing-in is to be as fun and stress free as possible - it is an excellent opportunity to dust off a role you've always loved or learn a new part that might not fit your usual archetype.
