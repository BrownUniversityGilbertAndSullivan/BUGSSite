The Sorcerer

Roles:

Sir Marmaduke Pointdextre - an Elderly Baronet (bass-baritone)
Alexis - of the Grenadier Guards & his Son (tenor)
Doctor Daly - Vicar of Ploverleigh (baritone)
John Wellington Wells - of J. W. Wells & Co., Family Sorcerers (patter baritone)
Lady Sangazure - a Lady of Ancient Lineage (alto)
Aline - her Daughter, betrothed to Alexis (soprano)
Mrs. Partlet - a Pew-opener (alto)
Constance - her Daughter (mezzo)
Notary (bass)

We will be performing the 1884 revival (not the original 1877 version) in its entirety.

 

Pirates of Penzance, or The Slave of Duty

Roles:

Major General Stanley (patter baritone)
The Pirate King (baritone)
Samuel - his Lieutenant (baritone)
Frederic - the Pirate Apprentice (tenor)
Sergeant of Police (bass)
Mabel (soprano)                            (General Stanley's Daughters)
Edith (mezzo-soprano)
Ruth - a Piratical Maid of all work (alto)

We will be performing the London work from No. 10 in the second act ("When a felon's not engaged in his employment") with the following changes: a) Following Ruth's "They are all noblemen who have gone wrong" we will include the quote from Pinafore ("What, all noblemen?"); b) Following Major General Stanley's "…because, with all our faults, we love our House of Peers" we will perform the brief Hymn to the Nobility from the New York version ("Hail; ever hail, O House of Peers!").
