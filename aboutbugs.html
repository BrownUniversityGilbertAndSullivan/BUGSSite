Brown University Gilbert & Sullivan, founded in 2004, is an undergraduate-led theater group devoted to producing high-quality Gilbert and Sullivan operas and related works, for the enjoyment of Brown students and the wider community.

BUGS is an all-volunteer organization, supported in part by university funds and in part through grants and private donations. We produce two shows every year and perform them with no admission charge.

We are one of the most welcoming theater groups you are likely to find, and always manage to produce incredible operettas while having tons of fun. Everyone from beginners to professional-track actors enjoy being in our shows.

BUGS is a fully-democratic organization, governed by a detailed Constitution, which spells out the mission of the group and the key positions. Each position is named in special BUGS fashion, using language taken from various G+S shows. The group is managed by a Board, known as the D’Oyly Carte (Gilbert and Sullivan’s impresario), with nine voting members and various associate members:
